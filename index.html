Who Is InTuition?

Tuition (n) - the act or profession of teaching.
Intuition (n) - immediate apprehension or cognition.

We are siblings.

Jacob - a Tallahasee native, a husband, and father of three. I completed a B.S. in Physics in 2008 and an M.S. in Science Education in 2010 both from Florida State University. For the past ten years I have been a full-time science and math teacher, and I love what I do. In my spare time I enjoy doing what I can to expand my knowledge and understanding of topics and hone my craft: working on coding projects, thinking about how to integrate programming into the courses I teach, and excellent professional development opportunities. A couple of my favorites were a week long experience at Fermilab (current home to Muon g-2, MicroBooNE, MINERvA, NOvA, and others) and a six week RET program at the National High Magnetic Field Laboratory where my group analyzed superconducting materials using an electron scanning microscope.

Joy - born and raised in Tallahassee. I love this town for its huge oak trees and abundant parks. I attended Florida State University where I double majored in Physical Science and Science Education, graduating in 2012. I taught 8th grade Physical Science and Lego Robotics at Maclay school right out of college, and I worked at the Challenger Learning Center as an Educator and Curriculum Developer for a total of 6 years. I took a break from teaching in 2014 to pursue a career in zookeeping. I received an A.S. in Zoo Animal Technology from Santa Fe College in Gainesville, FL. I most recently worked at Lion Country Safari as a Lion Keeper and Animal Trainer. In 2019 I decided to return to my beloved hometown to be near my family, especially my growing niece and nephews. I took a position at Community Christian School working in Educational Services where I teach individualized courses and tutor students, making sure they receive their accommodations. When I'm not teaching I enjoy exploring the woods, dogsitting, and expanding my knowledge and understanding of math and science through research and workshops like the RET at the Mag Lab and Quarknet's DataCamp.
